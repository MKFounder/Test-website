Full platform · 2026

The Mo for
Toronto vision

Toronto is too slow. Too closed. Too expensive to build in, live in, and start a business in. This platform is a set of concrete commitments with deadlines, accountability, and consequences if City Hall fails to deliver. Every policy is designed around one belief: every Torontonian deserves dignity, opportunity, and a city that works at the speed of real life.

Too slow

Housing approvals, business permits, service requests — Toronto moves at bureaucratic speed, not human speed.

Survival mode

Students, renters, founders, and workers are not thriving. They are surviving. That is not what a great city looks like.

City Hall should buy from local firms, pilot local ideas, and enable people to build — not get in the way.

01 — Core commitment

Housing acceleration

Toronto's housing crisis is partly a supply crisis and largely a speed crisis. Permits take months. Templates don't exist. Homeowners who want to add a suite can't figure out how. Mo will fix the machinery.

30
Day permit guarantee
Complete, compliant applications for low-rise, multiplex, garden suites, laneway suites, additions, and basement rental legalization get an approval or deficiency list within 30 days — or the City pays a fee rebate.
Make it easier to build up and build more
Core

Homeowners should be able to add floors, add units, build suites, and create small-scale housing — increasing supply, helping homeowners generate income, and allowing young people to stay in Toronto.

  • Pre-approved building templates so homeowners don't start from scratch
  • Full digital tracking of permit status — no more calling and waiting
  • Automatic conditional approval for certain compliant applications
  • Fee rebates if City misses the 30-day deadline
Student housing fast-track
Core

Make it easier for universities and colleges to build student housing in exchange for rent freezes or strict below-market rent commitments. Students should not be forced into survival mode just to study.

  • Fast-track approvals and reduced development charges for compliant projects
  • Density incentives tied to affordable rent commitments
  • Servicing priority for student housing projects near campuses
02 — Core commitment

Local economic growth & procurement reform

Toronto spends billions on city contracts — and most of that money leaves the local ecosystem. Mo will fix the flow. City Hall should be a platform for local firms, not a closed buyer.

20%
Local procurement mandate
20% of eligible City contracts — professional services, software, consulting, maintenance, cultural programming, and SME-accessible work — go to Toronto startups and companies that keep money in the local ecosystem.
Contract unbundling
Core

Break large City contracts into smaller pieces so startups, SMEs, and local firms can actually bid and win. The current system favours large incumbents by default.

Paid pilot pathway
Core

Create a city-startup pathway where local firms can pilot solutions with the City and then scale them. Winning ideas get integrated into city operations.

Procurement transparency dashboard
Core

A public dashboard showing where City dollars go, the local share of spending, vendor performance, and procurement outcomes. Sunlight is the best accountability mechanism.

03 — Core commitment

Small business platform

Small businesses are squeezed by rent, insurance, bylaw complexity, and permit delays — and the city keeps making it harder. Mo will make Toronto the easiest big city in North America to open, operate, and grow a small business.

48
Hour permit guarantee
Low-risk small business permits — signage, patios, minor interior renovations, low-risk event permits, basic operational approvals — approved within 48 hours or the applicant gets a fee rebate.
Small business impact assessment
Core

Any new City policy or bylaw must include a small business impact review before passing. No more regulation that looks good on paper and destroys local commerce in practice.

Small business resilience strategy
Core

Reduce friction across the board — bylaw complexity, permit delays, and administrative burden. This is not anti-worker. It is pro-business and pro-neighbourhood.

"Make Toronto the easiest big city in North America to open, operate, and grow a business."

Mo's commitment to small business owners
04 — Core commitment

Managed 24/7 city & night economy

Toronto is too closed. The night economy is being strangled by a system designed for a different era. Mo will build a managed, safe, open city.

24/7
Toronto After Dark strategy
Designated nightlife and entertainment zones with default extended hours. A managed night economy policy, not a chaos policy. Toronto should be open for business and open for life.
Extended hours zones
Core

Designated nightlife zones where extended hours are the norm. Simpler permitting for clubs, bars, events, and music venues. Lighting and safety infrastructure built into the design of night economy zones.

Safety-integrated night economy
Core

Late-night transit reliability. Noise mediation instead of purely punitive enforcement. Integration with crisis response and public safety planning. A city that's alive is a city designed well.

05 — Core commitment

AI modernization & civic tech

AI is a tool. Mo will use it to make City Hall faster, more responsive, and more accountable — and make sure every Torontonian, not just the tech-connected, can benefit.

"AI is not more important than potholes. It helps us get to potholes faster."

Mo's AI policy philosophy
AI modernization of City Hall
Core

Deploy AI to speed up permit processing, improve service routing, improve pothole and infrastructure response, triage complaints, improve FOI handling, and reduce administrative delays across every city department.

Ward-level civic innovation program
Core

Ward-by-ward civic problem-solving, culminating in a citywide challenge or summit. Winning ideas tied to paid pilot pathways. Real problems, real money, real deployment.

AI literacy for all Torontonians
Core

Public-facing AI education through libraries, universities, colleges, senior workshops, small business training, and workforce programs. Everyone should be able to use AI — not just elites.

06 — Core commitment

Youth, students & talent retention

Toronto trains some of the best talent in the world — then watches it leave. Mo will build the systems that make it realistic to build your life here.

"Toronto should be the best city in the world to start your life."

Mo's commitment to youth and students
Student-focused affordability
Core

Student housing fast-tracks tied to rent freezes and below-market commitments. Policy designed to ensure students have a step up — not to force them to live only to survive.

Talent retention doctrine
Core

The city should stop training talent and losing it. Targeted programs for students, young professionals, founders, creators, and builders to stay, grow, and contribute to the Toronto economy.

Student transit affordability
Ambitious goal

Targeted fare models, off-peak passes, and a "build here" transit retention pass for students, apprentices, and new graduates — reducing burden and making staying in Toronto realistic.

Civic fellowships and youth pipelines
Core

Targeted civic fellowships connecting young talent to city operations — modernization roles, youth advisory positions, and city talent pipelines that give young Torontonians a reason to stay.

07 — Supporting platform

City Hall productivity reform

City Hall has grown its administrative layer while frontline services stagnate. Mo will modernize the engine of city government.

Cap administrative growth
Core

Restrain administrative expansion without cutting frontline services. Reduce consultant reliance and build internal capacity. Stop paying outsiders to do what City staff should be empowered to do.

Full digitization of city processes
Core

Permits, licensing, service requests, FOI responses, and complaint tracking — all modernized, all trackable, all digital. Government should work like 2026.

Outcome-based program review
Core

Every program should be reviewed based on outcomes, impact, and measurable performance. Nothing runs forever just because it always has.

Public service standards dashboard
Core

A public-facing dashboard showing permit turnaround times, licensing turnaround, service request responsiveness, and key operational metrics. If Mo misses targets, Torontonians will know. That is the point.

08 — Values layer

Safety, dignity & lived experience

Mo is not anti-safety. The model is safety plus opportunity, safety plus liveliness, safety plus systems design. Stagnation is not safety.

Safety through better systems
Core

Investment in mental health crisis response, youth programming, and community-based prevention alongside enforcement. Better design produces better outcomes.

Beauty and dignity as policy
Values

Every human deserves dignity, respect, and a chance to build a purposeful life. Vibrant public spaces, well-lit streets, cultural energy, nightlife, and city design that feels alive — not deadened. Hope and beauty are not luxuries. They are what a great city provides.

Further ideas under development

The full idea bank

These ideas are part of the full platform conversation — ambitious, in need of stronger design, or being refined before full commitment. They reflect the scale of Mo's ambition for Toronto.

$1 student fares
Exploratory

A strong student mobilization idea with viral potential. Likely requiring targeting, phase-in, or off-peak limitations. The underlying principle — transit should not be a barrier to staying in Toronto — is non-negotiable.

Civic innovation fund
Ambitious goal

Milestone-based civic innovation grants for Toronto startups solving city problems. Prototype funding tied to paid pilot pathways — not startup cheques, but earned capital tied to outcomes.

Revenue expansion
Ambitious goal

Toronto has enormous underused economic capacity. Significantly grow the tax base through housing growth, local business activity, and modernization — without relying only on property tax increases.
