Campus movement

Students
for Mo

Toronto should be the best city in the world to start your life. Right now, it's not. Students are in survival mode — squeezed by rent, food costs, transit, and a city that wasn't designed with you in mind. Mo will change that.

The real issues

Why this matters
for students

01
Rent is impossible

Average rent for a one-bedroom in Toronto is over $2,400/month. Students are working two jobs just to study. The city has to stop pretending this is normal.

02
Transit is too expensive

$3.30 per ride adds up fast. Students who can't afford a monthly pass are choosing between transit and food. This is not acceptable in a world-class city.

03
The city is too closed

Nightlife, arts, culture, experimentation — the things that make cities worth living in — are being choked by permits, bylaws, and a city that closes at 11pm.

04
Nobody is hiring locally

Toronto startups and local firms can't get City contracts. The city buys from outsiders while students look for work. A 20% local procurement mandate changes that.

05
Talent leaves

Toronto trains some of the best graduates in the world — and watches them move to Toronto, Austin, London, or New York. The city needs to make staying realistic.

06
City Hall ignores you

Students are a massive constituency and almost entirely absent from city decision-making. Mo will change who's in the room when decisions are made.

Mo's commitments to students

What Mo
will do

Housing
Student housing fast-track

Fast-track approvals and reduced development charges for universities and colleges that commit to rent freezes or strict below-market caps. Students should not be forced into survival mode just to study.

Transit
Targeted transit affordability

Targeted fare models, off-peak passes, and a "build here" transit retention pass for students, apprentices, and new grads. The goal is to reduce burden and make staying in Toronto realistic.

Jobs & economy
20% local procurement mandate

20% of eligible City contracts go to Toronto companies. That means more local jobs, more local startups, and more reasons to build your career in Toronto instead of leaving for somewhere else.

Civic tech
Ward-level civic innovation program

A citywide innovation challenge tied to real paid pilot opportunities. Students and new grads can solve city problems and get paid to deploy them. City Hall becomes a platform for your ideas.

Night economy
Toronto After Dark

Extended hours zones. Simpler permits for arts, culture, and nightlife venues. A city that's actually alive — because students, artists, and young workers deserve a city that doesn't close at 11pm.

AI literacy
AI for everyone, not just elites

Public AI education through libraries, colleges, and universities. Mo's view: AI will define the next economy. Every student should have access to the tools and training to compete.
